World Population

The population of the entire world as of today is 8.2 billion. It is estimated that we have had a 0.9% increase during each annual change as of 2023.

Affecting Factors

We've mentioned before some factors that effect population, both in areas and in general. The world population tends to follow one of the same reasons: Birth rates. This includes both the increases/decreases, and is affected by multiple factors such as:

  • Healthcare
  • Abortions
  • Social Factors
  • Cost of living

Healthcare

Healthcare is one of the biggest factors that affects birth rates across the world. It is what assists mothers during times of labor, and so on. Healthcare is crucial to the world, because it helps with births by increasing its rates. Without it, hundreds of thousands of embryos wouldn't even become babies.

Abortions

Another huge factor of birth rates is abortions. Abortions are the killing of babies before they are even born. This plays a negative role in the world, because it leads to birth rates decreasing, and innocent lives being lost. Some reasons a woman in labor might want to do this is because of their own health, the stress of having to deal with a baby, and so on.

Social Factors

Social factors that affect birth rates include marriages and divorces. When couples are married, this allows an increase to birth rates, which is a big plus. Divorces, on the other hand, follow stress as it leads to the break up of two individuals who at one point swore themselves to love each other. Divorces can have a big impact on any children the couple had (if they had any), because their children would grow up with that memory, and potentially not seek for marriage.

Cost of living

The final factor, cost of living, impacts families. If they could not afford shelter or food (if they could a poor ammount), they wouldn't be able to afford a healthy lifestlye. This also means that, if the mother was in labor, couldn't safely give birth to children, as there would be a high chance of death if the woman did it out of a hospital

These factors overall affect our world's population, with both positive and negative effects.
