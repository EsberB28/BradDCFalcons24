Population

What is population?

Population is the entirety of inhabitants occupying a certain area (different countries or just the world in general) which is always changed and modified. This is done based on increasing (births and immigration) or decreasing (deaths or emigration) modifications. Since the older years, the world's population has surpassed 8 billion people. At 1 A.D., the population was only at about 300 million, and by the 1800s, it reached 1 billion.

So... what is this use of knowledge useful for? Why is it important to know population density? What does it even mean?

Well... knowing and understanding the population densities of different areas are very important, because they hold the given information that tells the government what necessities are needed to be met for everyone in that area to stay alive. This means that the government provides different areas with different ammounts of food based on how many people live in that area.

Another reason the government uses population density data is to identify the overall ammount of people from each culture who live in an area. They also determine more specific details, such as their age, sex, occupation, language, and so on. Population density is a fancy way to say the counting of people in different areas.


Population is something crucial to interpret, because it allows higher officials to make decisions accordingly that would best fit a given area, and affects how people live. Since it also specifies on people's background information, it allows the government to build infrastructures accordingly (such as schools, hospitals, and grocery stores). Population also allows the government to plan ahead based on the trends they see in the data. This means that they are making decisions based on an incline or decline in population that would best benefit the future of that area.

Population also affects how others live, based on the ammount of inhabitants in a specific area. The more people living in an area, the more tangibles they'll need to survive, and the less people livng in an area, they less they need. A larger population would also mean smaller houses because of the ammount of people living in that area, while a smaller population would mean more room to live in larger households.
