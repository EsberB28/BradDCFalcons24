Mission

“Develop the Game, Touch the World, Build a Better Future”

Vision

Make soccer a fan-favorite nationwide by creating events which would gather hundreds of thousands of fans.

Goals

To promote soccer all around the world, and make it a world-renowned sport. The founders wanted it to have a positive impact over society, allowing it to bring everyone together, and have a good time.

History

Fifa (Federation Internationale de Football Association) was founded in 1904 by Belgium, Denmark, France, Netherlands, Spain, Sweden and Switzerland. International games began growing at the dawn of the 20th century because of them, and so fifa formed a governing body, with Robert Guerin, a French journalist, as its first ever president. The 2014 World Cup in Brazil was broadcast in more than 200 countries. Inside the stadiums, a total of 3,429,873 spectators attended the 64 matches – an average of 53,592 per match

Achievments

Fifa managed to establish one of the most anticipating 4-year events in history. Every year, millions of spectators and fans gather to watch their favorite soccer players/teams dominate the field, and compete for the trophy. This is one of the biggest sporting events in history, as the world cup gathers countries to go head-to-head.

Notable Events

Fifa's biggest notable events include creating the following:

  • FIFA World Cup
  • FIFA Women's World Cup

Each of the clubs above include games between different countries, which consist of 45 minutes, a half time, and 45 more minutes (with a few minutes of extra time at the end). The founders made it so that if a match was tied until the end, there would be penalties until a winner rises.
